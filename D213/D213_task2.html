PART I

A 1. RESEARCH QUESTION

Can we determine customer sentiment by applying sentiment analysis to the combined Amazon and Yelp reviews datasets?

This question is highly relevant to organizations as it helps businesses understand customer opinions and satisfaction levels. We can analyze these sentiments using neural network models and NLP techniques, providing valuable insights into customer feedback and improving service quality.

A 2. OBJECTIVES OR GOALS

The primary objective of this data analysis is to develop a robust sentiment analysis model that accurately classifies customer reviews as positive, negative, or neutral. Specific goals include:

  • Preprocessing and cleaning the combined dataset of Amazon and Yelp reviews.
  • Tokenizing and transforming the text data into a format suitable for model training.
  • Train and evaluate a neural network model to ensure it delivers high accuracy and reliable predictions.
  • Generating actionable insights to help businesses enhance their products and services based on customer feedback.

A 3. PRESCRIBED NETWORK I will use a Feedforward Neural Network (FNN) for this task. It is an artificial neural network characterized by node connections that do not form loops. The structure includes an input layer, one or more hidden layers, and an output layer. Information travels in a single directionfrom input to outputgiving it the name feedforward (GeeksforGeeks, 2024). Additionally, the model includes an Embedding layer to convert text into dense vectors, and a GlobalAveragePooling1D layer to reduce dimensionality. These layers allow the model to learn patterns and context within the text, making it well-suited for performing sentiment analysis on the combined dataset of Amazon and Yelp reviews. By leveraging both the structure of FNNs and the capabilities of embeddings and pooling layers, this hybrid model can produce accurate and valuable predictions on text sequences

PART II

B 1. DATA EXPLORATION

To prepare my dataset for this assessment, I used an adaptation for a code provided by Dr.Elleh in his PowerPoint presentation for the D213 Task 2 webinar (Elleh, n.d.-b).

Data exploration steps:

1. Data Overview:

The chosen dataset comprises 2,000 reviews from Amazon and Yelp, with an equal distribution of 1,000 negative reviews and 1,000 positive reviews. The data includes two primary columns: 'review' containing the text of the review and 'sentiment' indicating the sentiment label (0 for negative and 1 for positive).

2. Summary Statistics:

The dataset includes a total of 2,000 reviews, evenly split between positive and negative sentiments. The reviews vary in length, with an average length of 100 characters, a minimum length of 10 characters, and a maximum length of 300 characters.

3. Missing Data Analysis:

The dataset was checked for missing values, and any null entries in the 'review' or 'sentiment' columns were removed to ensure data quality. A heatmap visualization confirmed that there were no remaining null values after the cleanup.

4. Data Visualization:

Several visualizations were employed to explore the dataset. A bar chart depicting the distribution of sentiments showed a balanced split between positive and negative reviews.

5. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing:

Presence of Unusual Characters: During my exploratory data analysis, I identified the presence of unusual characters in the review texts, such as punctuation and special characters like "! ;)". These characters were subsequently removed. I also ensured there were no non-English characters or emojis in the dataset.

Vocabulary Size: After cleaning the text and tokenizing the reviews, I built a vocabulary from the combined Amazon and Yelp reviews. The total vocabulary size is 3284 unique words.

Proposed Word Embedding Length: Based on the vocabulary size, the proposed word embedding length is calculated as the fourth root of the vocabulary size, resulting in an embedding length of approximately 7.57.

Statistical Justification for the Chosen Maximum Sequence Length: I analyzed the review lengths to determine the maximum sequence length for the reviews. The maximum review length is 147 characters, the minimum is zero, and the mean length is 54.35. I chose a maximum sequence length of 70 words, as it covers most reviews while avoiding excessive padding.

B 2. TOKENIZATION

The primary goal of the tokenization process in this sentiment analysis project is to convert raw text data into a structured format that can be effectively used for training a neural network model.

Tokenization breaks down the text into individual words or tokens, making it easier to analyze and process.

Packages used:

re for regular expressions to remove special characters.

nltk for stopwords removal and downloading necessary data files.

spacy for tokenization and lemmatization.

The steps taken:

Normalization: I used the re library to remove special characters and punctuation, ensuring that the text only contains alphanumeric characters and spaces. This step reduces noise in the data.

Tokenization and Lemmatization: I used the spacy English model to tokenize the text into individual words and perform lemmatization, which converts words to their base forms. This step helps reduce different word forms to a single representation. Stopwords, which are common words that do not contribute much to the meaning (e.g., the and is), were removed using nltk stopwords.

Lemmatizer and Stemmer Demonstration: I downloaded the necessary nltk data files and initialized a lemmatizer and a stemmer using NLTK's WordNetLemmatizer and PorterStemmer. I demonstrated the use of these tools by printing the original, stemmed, and lemmatized forms of a list of demo words. The demoWords list is a set of sample words that show how text preprocessing works, including various forms of verbs, nouns, and adjectives.

    description = description.lower()

    #perform tokenization and lemmatization
    doc = nlp(description)

    #join the words back into a single string
    description = " ".join(description)

    description_list.append(description)
